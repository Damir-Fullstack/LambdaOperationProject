λ-Operation

The λ-operation is a mathematical function performed over a matrix system. It combines the sum of all column means and the overall system mean to produce a unique numerical result.

Working with Negative Numbers

In this system, calculations for negative ranges do not start from zero. To maintain the mathematical integrity of the matrix, the count begins at -1. This prevents rows from being nullified and preserves the structure of the operation.

The Rule of Symmetry

A key discovery in this operation is its non-commutative nature. The order of operands A and B is critical. For example, the result for λ(10, 6) is different from λ(6, 10). This asymmetry proves that each variable plays a distinct role in the matrix construction.

Universal Scale and Decimals

The operation is not limited to integers. It supports fractional values (e.g., 1.5) by creating a proportional scaling of the columns. This allows the λ-function to exist as a continuous mathematical model.
